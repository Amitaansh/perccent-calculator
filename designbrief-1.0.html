Project Overview
Subject

A unified financial calculator that lets a customer model SIP, Lumpsum, Step-up SIP, SWP and EMI in one place — with every input exposed as a control and every output explained.

Audience

Retail Indian investors comparing options before committing — first-time SIP starters through to people planning withdrawals (SWP) or loans (EMI). Numerically curious, mobile-and-desktop, trust-sensitive.

The screen's single job

Let someone change an assumption and instantly see — and understand — what it does to their money. Confidence through clarity, not just a final number.

Why we win

Groww, Zerodha & bank calculators show a number and hide the math. Perccent shows the number and the formula, the assumptions, and the year-by-year breakdown behind it.

★ Signature element — the Transparency Panel

Every result card carries an expandable panel that reveals the exact formula used, the compounding/timing assumption (annuity-due vs ordinary, effective vs nominal rate), and an editable assumptions footnote. This is the one idea repeated across all five modes and the thing Perccent should be remembered for.

Chart rules

05
Inputs & Micro-interactions

The signature control is the slider-plus-value-chip pair, lifted from the best of Groww/Zerodha but improved: the chip is directly editable (type an exact figure), and the slider snaps to sensible steps. Every change recalculates in real time with a number count-up — never a jarring jump.

Slider + editable value chip
Monthly investment₹5,000
Expected return (p.a.)12%
Time period10 yr

Interaction rules

  • Slider drag and chip edit are always in sync — change one, the other follows.
  • Out-of-range typed values clamp to min/max with an inline hint, never an error modal.
  • Recalculation is debounced (~120ms) so dragging stays smooth; results count-up over ~400ms.
  • All controls keyboard-accessible; sliders respond to arrow keys with the same step.

Motion & Animation Principles

Motion exists to make recalculation legible, never to perform. The rule of thumb: the more often a customer triggers a motion, the faster and quieter it must be.

MomentFrequencyTreatment
Result count-upEvery input change~400ms ease-out number tween; chart arcs morph in sync. No bounce.
Slider dragConstantInstant, debounced ~120ms. Zero animation on the knob itself.
Mode switchOccasional200–250ms cross-fade between panels; chart type morphs, doesn't pop.
Transparency Panel openOccasionalHeight auto-expand 220ms ease; formula fades in.
SWP depletion-to-zeroRare (per scenario)Most expressive moment — the line draws and the zero-marker pulses once.

Non-negotiable: every animation respects prefers-reduced-motion and falls back to an instant state change.

11
Do / Don't
✓ Do
+Expose every assumption — always show the timing (annuity-due vs ordinary) and rate (effective vs nominal) convention in plain language.
+Keep one primary blue button per view; everything else is secondary or ghost.
+Use JetBrains Mono for every rupee figure, percentage and table cell so numbers align.
+Keep the semantic triad consistent: invested = blue, returns = green, withdrawn/interest = amber, on every mode.
+Recalculate live and count-up — never make the user press "calculate" to see a change.
✕ Don't
–Don't hide the math — a number with no visible formula is the exact thing we're replacing.
–Don't use pure black — always Ink #050606 (and its dark-mode equivalent).
–Don't use green for anything that isn't growth/returns, or amber for anything that isn't withdrawal/interest.
–Don't animate frequent interactions (drag, count-up) longer than ~400ms.
–Don't show a maturity figure without stating it's pre-tax and free of exit load, where relevant.
